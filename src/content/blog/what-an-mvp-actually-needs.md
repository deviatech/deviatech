The founders who come to us for an MVP almost always arrive with a full feature list — the whole roadmap, not just the first step. That's normal; it's your vision. But building all of it before you've tested the core assumption is usually the slowest way to find out if you have a real business.

## The one question that matters first

Before anything else, we ask: **what's the one thing this product needs to prove?** Not "what should it eventually do" — what does it need to prove *right now*, to the first 20-50 real users.

Everything that isn't in service of that answer gets pushed to a "phase two" list. Not deleted — just not in the way of getting a real signal fast.

## What usually survives the cut

- The single core flow (the thing users actually came for)
- Basic auth, if you need to know who's using it
- Just enough admin to see what's happening, not a full dashboard
- A way to actually collect feedback, even if it's just your own WhatsApp number in the footer

## What usually gets deferred

- Multiple user roles and permissions
- Notification systems beyond the essential one
- Payment integrations, unless payment *is* the core assumption being tested
- Polish that doesn't affect whether the core flow works

None of this means cutting corners on quality — it means being disciplined about what "done" means for a first version. A working, honest MVP beats an unfinished version of everything.
